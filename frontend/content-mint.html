Why mint?

Because screenshots aren't ownership. Because cloud folders aren't protection. Because your content deserves your chain.

Minting isn't just tech — it's a statement. It says: "This is mine. And no one touches it unless I say so." In Web3, your content becomes code — signed, sealed, and timestamped on-chain. No more blurry copyright notices. No more hidden folders or empty threats.

Minting means proof. It means your work can't be copied, claimed, or stolen without a fight they'll lose. It's your creation, your name, your rules — recorded forever in the blocks.

If you post without minting, you're handing it out unguarded. If you mint, you anchor it to the future. You don't just share. You show up — with proof of authorship, proof of existence, proof of intent.
